Introduction To Micro Frontends

  • Remember Mabunda
  • Jan 27, 2023

Introduction

Hi guys, Remember here, Senior Software Engineer at Param Solutions. This article is about the introduction to Micro Frontends. Micro frontends is an architecture pattern for developing and deploying web applications as smaller, independent, and decoupled components. The goal is to break down a monolithic frontend into smaller, more manageable parts that can be developed, tested, and deployed independently. This allows for increased collaboration, faster delivery, and improved scalability. The micro frontends communicate with each other through APIs or an integration layer and are combined to form a unified user experience. This approach can also help to mitigate technical debt, reduce complexity, and make it easier to maintain the application over time.

Benefits of using Micro Frontends

By breaking down a monolithic frontend into smaller, decoupled components, micro frontends provide a solution to these problems. This approach enables faster delivery, improved scalability, increased collaboration, and better maintainability, leading to a more efficient and effective development process.

  1. Improved scalability: Micro frontends allow for easier scaling of individual components, which can be optimized and deployed independently.
  2. Faster delivery: With smaller and more focused components, teams can work on and deliver new features faster.
  3. Increased collaboration: Micro frontends encourage cross-functional collaboration, as teams can work on different parts of the application in parallel.
  4. Better maintainability: Smaller, decoupled components make it easier to identify and fix problems, reducing the complexity of the overall system.
  5. Mitigated technical debt: Breaking down a monolithic application into smaller components can help to avoid technical debt and reduce the risk of problems in the future.
  6. Enhanced user experience: By allowing for targeted and optimized delivery of specific functionality, micro frontends can lead to a better user experience.
  7. Reusability: Micro frontends can be reused across different parts of the application, reducing development time and effort.

Why using Micro frontends?

Micro frontends are used to solve some of the common challenges faced by monolithic frontend applications, such as:

  1. Complexity: Monolithic frontends can become complex over time, making it difficult to maintain and scale the application.
  2. Slow delivery: The large size of monolithic frontends can slow down development and deployment of new features.
  3. Lack of collaboration: Monolithic frontends can lead to siloed development, reducing collaboration between teams.
  4. Technical debt: Over time, the technical debt in monolithic frontends can become significant and challenging to manage.
This was just introduction to Micro frontends, more articles are still coming, the goal is to show how to integrate Micro frontends with Microservices in the back end. Thanks for reading.
