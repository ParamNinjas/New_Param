Introduction To Microservices

  • Remember Mabunda
  • Jan 20, 2023

Hi guys, Remember here, Senior Software Engineer at Param Solutions. I have over six of experience working with Microservices. This is a brief introduction to Microservices. Brace yourselves for more articles about the topic this year.

Microservices is an architectural style for building software systems that consists of small, independent, modular services that communicate with each other over a network. Each service is responsible for a specific business capability and can be developed, deployed, and scaled independently of the other services.

The main idea behind microservices is to decompose a monolithic application into a collection of small, loosely coupled services that can be developed, deployed, and scaled independently. This allows for greater flexibility, scalability, and maintainability compared to a monolithic architecture, where all functionality is contained in a single codebase.

Microservices are commonly used in cloud-native and distributed systems and are often built using technologies such as containerization and service meshes. They are also commonly used in agile development as they allow for faster development and deployment cycles and better scalability.

While Microservices architecture can be beneficial, it also comes with its own set of challenges such as inter-service communication, service discovery, service registry, and security, so it's important to be aware of and prepared for these before implementing a microservices architecture.

We’ll discuss more about topic in the next coming articles, I’m so excited about this journey I can’t wait to dig deeper into the topic. Thanks for reading.
